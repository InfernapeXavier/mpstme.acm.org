Game Development

Brought to you by Popular Demand

The game development workshop was a first-of-its-kind event. The focus was on fostering the students’ interest in coding by making them solve real world problems and helping them learn what goes into making the games they love.

The attendees of the workshop learnt the basics of game development using professional tools: unity and c#. The three-day workshop served as the beginning of a career in game development for many Students. The attendees got a glimpse at the development cycle of a typical, professionally made game, and through a mix of practice and discussions, learnt the various aspects of the gaming engines being taught.

  • 2017: 100+ Registrations

The A{C}M Workshop

The Flagship

The C Workshop aims to take teaching to a level above the Curriculum while strengthening concepts and maintaing quality coding standards.

Spanning over 3 days with an audience of over 120, the workshop was held parallely across 2 rooms. It covered the curriculum in a systematic manner with practical examples taken up after each topic.

  • 2015: 50+   Registrations
  • 2016: 70+   Registrations
  • 2017: 200+ Registrations

Hour Of Code

The one for the Community

It all started with a movement by Code.org that aimed to educate students from the age of 4-104 about coding during World Computer Science Week. We saw it as a great opportunity to spread the culture in any and all schools we could get to. The result was an astounding number of registrations from 5 schools.

We conducted a 1 hour workshop according to code.org guidelines and finished a couple of logic building exercises that left the students enlightnened.

  • 2017: 80+ Registrations
